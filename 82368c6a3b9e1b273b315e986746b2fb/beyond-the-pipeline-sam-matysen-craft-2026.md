# Beyond the Pipeline – Sam Matysen (Talk Outline)

> A talk (with Allianz Technology) on becoming a truly data-driven enterprise — moving *beyond the pipeline* so data informs decisions, treating data as a **product**, not a project.

---

## 1. Why Data Is Special

- Data is **transparent** (a footprint of the company), **inclusive** (generated and consumed by everyone), and **enabling** (increasingly so in the AI age — not just integrations/dashboards but fast decisions/insights).

## 2. What Goes Wrong

- **Silos** → duplicated near-identical systems and **scattered logic** signed off by different parts of the business that never talk.
- **Tech-first** thinking (data as an IT project) → great town-hall demos but **low adoption / unclear ROI**.
- **Governance as an afterthought** → shaky foundations, compliance risk, no **ownership**.
- **Project (not product) operating model** → initiatives bleed out as the business moves on.

## 3. Guiding Principles

- **Federated governance** (shared framework, agreed base elements — not one central authority), accounting for varied **maturity** (finance strong, sales historically weak).
- **Domain-driven data ownership** — the bridge between business and IT that keeps data products aligned; clear responsibilities and yearly targets on both sides.
- **Composability** (common approach, don't reinvent the wheel) and **evolutionary architecture** (robust to change, not perfect).

## 4. The Phased Approach

- Start from **enterprise architecture** (business/information/application/technology), then pick **one capability + value stream** with the **biggest business impact** and map the data initiative to it. Define **business outcomes** (not dashboards/chatbots).
- **Phase 0 — strategic anchoring:** workshops with leadership *and* the whole team; a hard milestone — **every team member can articulate the transformation goal** without technical/solution language.
- **Design & build:** establish **domain ownership** early, design **data contracts/interfaces** (automation + transparency), focus on **data quality, lineage, observability**, and capture **context** for AI use cases.
- **Operating-model evolution:** project → **product delivery**, cross-functional teams, feedback loops, and governance **from gate to garden** (rules/roles that **enable** rather than limit — "what do people need to decide?").
- **Scaling:** extract a reusable **playbook** from the documented decisions, invest in **communication/marketing** and **upskilling** (become a trusted advisor) → network effects.

## 5. Real Example (Allianz commercial data platform)

- A 2–3-year platform with stalled adoption → restart. Pilot capability: **Sales & Distribution / customer acquisition** (goal: +2% medium-sized customers by year end). Built a **Customer 360** domain, set up stewards/champions, parameterized (not declarative) pipelines on Databricks for scalability/observability, then expanded to new data products (account graphs, portfolio health). Now in an exciting **scaling** phase with proactive business demand.

## 6. Takeaways

- **Data engineers/analysts:** think **beyond the pipeline** — understand the business capability you enable; **design for reuse** (contracts, self-service); **document everything**.
- **Architects/tech leaders:** treat data as a **first-class citizen**; governance **enables**, not blocks; mind the **glue** (metadata, lineage, context, observability).
- **Business leaders:** **co-own** the data product; start with success/business terms, not solutions; **fund products, not projects**.
- Surprising lesson: data can't integrate when the **business** isn't integrated (mergers/acquisitions) — **business transformation must precede data transformation** ("data is the footprint").
