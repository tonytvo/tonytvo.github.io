# The Eight Desires Workshop – Veronica Lynn Clark (Workshop Outline)

> A rare non-technical, interactive **personal-development workshop** at a tech conference — Veronica Lynn Clark guides attendees to know themselves better and intentionally design a more fulfilled life. *(A workshop, not a talk; this outlines its structure.)*

---

## 1. Framing

- People attended for varied reasons (curious, live experience, "know myself better," happiness/joy). Knowing yourself lets you use your **gifts** — innate talents so natural we don't recognize them.
- Tagline: **"A meaningful life is created intentionally."** Emotions will come up; the journey goes down before it goes up. "When you dig for gold, it's not on top."

## 2. The Wheel — Eight Life Domains

Born from Clark's 2009 breakdown and rebuilding, the wheel has eight domains (each with four descriptor adjectives):
- **Sexuality** (life force / aliveness, not just sex), **Recreation** (playfulness, "to re-create"), **Friends/Family/Community**, **Physical & Mental Health** (vibrancy, movement — "the purpose of life is to be expressed/alive"), **Money & Wealth** (generosity/abundance vs. scarcity), **Spirituality & Personal Growth** (self-awareness), **Romantic Relationships** (separate from sexuality; inner magnetism), **Career & Business** (sustainable impact, using your gifts — "directly connected to your magic and your money").

## 3. The Process (interactive, with journaling + music + movement)

1. **Score** each domain 1–5 (5 = thriving plant, 1 = significantly neglected) — honestly, based on how it *feels* to you, not comparison to others; face resistance/avoidance.
2. **Pick a 1 or 2** to work on.
3. **Dream** — imagine that domain fully **transformed** ("change the form / move from one state to another): how would you feel, what would be different? Write with a "magical pen," out of the head and into the body.
4. **Current reality** — honestly write: *What is the problem? What stories are you telling (about yourself, others, the situation)? What is it costing you?* (Stories = beliefs; "the operating system created by your 5-year-old self.")
5. **Create a new reality** — *What do I want to believe now?* Write a **realistic belief for today** plus a bigger one, and **one action within 24 hours** (and whose help you need). Adopt a **"get-to" attitude**, judgment-free.
6. **Share in small pods** — domain, imagined transformed life, next action, help needed.

## 4. Themes & Takeaways

- **Everything is interconnected** — one neglected domain sends "tentacles" into the others.
- The **comfort of the familiar** (even when dissatisfying) keeps us stuck; naming a problem aloud shrinks it.
- The coder analogy: journaling is **evaluating your own inner "code"** — unedited writing surfaces the unconscious/subconscious beliefs so you can **rewrite them**.
- **We're not lazy** — we do the best we can with our resources; change takes courage. Confidence = *confide* (with trust) → trusting yourself → showing up more → becoming more magnetic.
- **"See the next step, not the mountain."** You get to rewrite your program every day — go live the best life you can, because you get to. (Group work also modeled teamwork/vulnerability for the engineers present.)
