# AI & I: The Architecture of Our Identity – Xin Yao (Workshop Outline)

> A **Craft 2026** interactive workshop (~20–30 people, 90 minutes) by **Xin Yao**, a socio-technical architect who "goes around highlighting connections" (the yellow connector pen) and facilitates the first phase of transformations at companies "with a lot of people, a lot of software, and a lot of complexity." The inquiry: **who am I when knowledge — our former core competency — is being commoditized by AI?** Rather than a talk, she models "unlikely, awkward, high-stakes conversations" experientially — "making knowledge relational, not transactional." The workshop's own arc: a fist-finger check-in → *connection before content* (the VIEW model) → a *head/heart/gut* identity architecture → a *possibles / lovables / undiscussables* conversation café → a themes-and-tensions harvest → integration. Participants have "100% choice and control over the level of vulnerability" throughout.

---

## 1. Opening — The Identity Inquiry

### 1.1 An experiment for contribution
- Framed as a **discovery experience plus a learning experiment**; the design "could work for you or work poorly for this room."
- Everyone agrees to be "up for experiments today."

### 1.2 Identity as the theme
- In IT "we all work with identifiers" — **identity** is the distinguishing characteristic that "makes me me and Simon Simon and you you."

### 1.3 Knowledge being commoditized
- "We are all **knowledge professionals**" — we work with knowledge.
- Per **Gergely's keynote** and **Wardley maps**, knowledge is being pushed into the **commodity range** — including analyzing, rating, even **creative** knowledge activities.
- The question: if these former **core competencies / expertise** are commoditized, "what is our defining characteristic tomorrow?"

## 2. Check-in — The "Collaborative Calculator"

Participants raise a fist and put up one finger per question they identify with.

### 2.1 Q1 — Daily AI use
- Who uses AI on a **daily / almost daily** basis? (First finger.)

### 2.2 Q2 — Efficiency excitement vs. time-deprivation/addiction
- Excited that AI **saves time / efficiency gains**, yet still feeling **time-deprived and addicted**.

### 2.3 Q3 — Cognitive boost vs. dependency/erosion
- Excited about AI's **cognitive-boosting** effects (smarter, more creative), yet worried about **dependency and cognitive erosion**.

### 2.4 Q4 — Solo efficiency vs. missing social grounding
- Excited by **solo efficiency** (no longer depending on others for knowledge), yet **missing the social grounding** of talking it through with someone.

### 2.5 Q5 — Role/responsibility clarity
- Feeling a **lack of clarity about roles and responsibilities**.

### 2.6 The result
- Most people raise **multiple fingers** (some five, four, a few threes) — "thank you for your honesty."
- These ambivalences are exactly what the session unpacks.

## 4. Connection Before Content — The VIEW Model

### 4.1 The first connection is to ourselves
- "Connection" first evokes people, but the **first connection we need is to ourselves** — often lost even in keynotes.

### 4.2 The intention compass
- Draw a **compass** on a sticky, put **one word** in the center for your **intention** in the session — how you want to participate/contribute.
- Xin Yao's word: **"courage"** — a subtle, vulnerable topic in a compact, complex format.

### 4.3 VIEW — introduced by Joe Hudson
- **VIEW** (Joe Hudson) models our **connectedness to ourselves**; score your *intention* (1–10) on each — "want to be" is an intention, not a guarantee (like a Christmas wish list).

### 4.4 V — Vulnerability
- **Speak my truth even though it's scary** — "say the scary thing *because* it is my truth."
- Question: **How willing am I to be seen** in the room?

### 4.5 I — Impartiality
- Have a **clear want** for the session **without an agenda** — not forcing it on yourself or others.
- Question: **How willing am I to be influenced / moved?**

### 4.6 E — Empathy
- Be **present with another's emotion without taking it on**.
- Question: **How willing am I to listen** just to get the other person?

### 4.7 W — Wonder
- **Curiosity without needing to know the answer**.
- Question: **How willing am I to be in wonder** and not "in the know"?

### 4.8 The trio VIEW conversation
- Groups of three explore: **"What about AI is making you feel a little weird or wild about being you right now?"**
- **15 seconds** of productive silence first; the **person with the longest hair** starts by asking the question; each person gets ~**1.5 minutes** while others practice **listening**.

## 6. Identity Architecture — Head / Heart / Gut

### 6.1 The tug-of-war in the mud
- A **tug-of-war**: on one side, knowledge professionals want **self-expression, creativity, and work-life balance**; on the other, the world (per the OpenAI-flavored keynote) wants **ruthless productivity, endless output, and endless adaptation**.
- We stand "in the mud holding the rope while **AI shifts the ground underneath us**."

### 6.2 "Who are we when the machine learns our craft?"
- Left in the air at a conference called **Craft**.

### 6.3 The Matrix — "Am I the one?"
- The famous **Oracle's-kitchen** scene where **Neo** asks "**Am I the one?**" — a **binary question** with only two answers.
- When identity is **threatened, thinking regresses to binary** ("will I stay relevant in the age of AI?").
- **Neo** lived a double life (programmer by day, hacker by night); **Morpheus** told him he'd save the world, but he's anxious to leave his identity behind.

### 6.4 Grieving the old identity
- Leaving behind an old identity of self-expression/creativity **requires grieving** — Neo won't grieve "unless you tell me I'm the one."

### 6.5 The four F's — including "feed"
- In fear we get **fight, flight, freeze**, and a fourth added for the age of AI: **feed** — instead of stopping when tired, "just one more prompt… that dopamine fix around the corner."

### 6.6 The three-circle learning-loop trick
- She invites imagining **three empty circles** (Venn diagram) — as knowledge professionals, "your brain does not want to leave those circles empty," hooking attention (a learning trick).

### 6.7 Head — Capability
- **Head = capability**: what I know, my role, craft, skill.
- This is where **fear-based marketing targets us** — the flood of podcasts, AI newsletters, and courses you feel you *must* click "or my craft won't develop."

### 6.8 Heart — Capacity / Connection
- **Heart = capacity/connection**: connection to self, others, and the **bigger picture** — impact, resources, support system, agency.
- Companies with the **best-skilled** people can still deliver **mediocre performance** (she's left such companies); startups with less money but real collaboration "move the needle."

### 6.9 Gut — Core (safety and pleasure)
- **Gut = core**: both **safety** and **pleasure**.
- A coach's line: "**you can only rise to the height of your lowest system**" — if safety is threatened, the frontal cortex won't activate.
- Also **pleasure**: which parts (prompting, context engineering, linting, harnessing) do you do out of **fear** vs. **enjoyment**? Enjoyment feels **light, soft, gentle** ("I don't want it to end"); non-enjoyment feels **tense, heavy, weighty**.

### 6.10 Craft is only ~1/3 of our humanity
- The pulsating **"who am I?"** sits in the middle; **craft/ability is only about one-third** of our humanity — "that's not the whole story."

### 6.11 Identity as never-ending recalibration
- Success with expertise (she teaches **DDD / domain-driven design and architecture**) tempts us to fix identity to expertise — but "**life is a never-ending process of identity recalibration**," painful on a visceral level.

### 6.12 Emotional fluidity underpins identity fluidity
- Shedding our "snake skin" of past success requires **emotional fluidity** — the capacity to feel all emotions and let them flow.
- Neuroscientifically, we make decisions **to avoid uncomfortable emotions** (the FOMO AI-course signup) — these are "**kinks in the water flow**" that block us from seeking what we actually want, making us follow **someone else's model**.

## 9. Conversation Café — Possibles / Lovables / Undiscussables

### 9.1 Framing — be selfish, no correct answers
- Model your group's **exact configuration** — "be super selfish"; don't model roles not in your group.
- "There is no correct answer" — feel the lightness/weirdness and enjoy it.

### 9.2 Possibles (blue) — roles and AI use cases
- Like **event storming / command storming**: an event is "agent-prompted"; a **command/use case** is "prompt agent" — a **verb in active imperative form**.
- Format: "**I, the [role], use AI to [verb] [object]**" (e.g., "I, the PM, use AI to analyze user research").
- Process: **1 minute silent** individual brainstorming (yellow = roles, blue = use cases), then **5 minutes** group modeling; then **mark use cases that can be collaborative** (done as a team with the LLMs).

### 9.3 Lovables (green) — deeper need + emotion
- A combination of **user-need mapping and emotional inquiry**.
- **L = logical**: the **deeper need** beneath a use case, found via the **five whys** (e.g., "I use LLMs to answer emails" → *why?* → the real need).
- **E = emotional**: "**I feel ___ when this need is met**" (adventurous, accomplished, safe, boosted…), building an **emotional vocabulary**.
- At least **two greens per blue** (one logical, one emotional).

### 9.4 Undiscussables (red) — the elephants
- Pick use cases that evoke a **worry, dilemma, or uncomfortable feeling**.
- Go beyond the socially acceptable to your **authentic truth**: "what makes you uncomfortable that you haven't shared — maybe not even with yourself?"
- **30 seconds** silent reflection, then a group conversation with **curiosity and empathy switches on**.

## 10. Themes & Tensions — Hypothesis-Based Harvest

### 10.1 The tension masters
- Instead of a gallery walk, a **hypothesis-based harvest**, documented live by two volunteer co-facilitators: **Simon** (themes) and **Alex** (tensions) — "with legible handwriting."

### 10.2 Hypothesis 1 — Productivity use cases
- **Increase productivity** (product designers/managers — noted as sounding more like a *need*).
- **Documentation** (assembling docs with AI).
- **Research / searching** (user research, "finding things faster").
- **Writing code** and **creating tests** (a teacher/mentor role — for **speed**).
- **Debugging issues / incidents** — faster resolution pulling data from graphs and analytics.
- **Break the effort wall / make people commit** (a **solutions architect**) — user need: **prevent procrastination**; "excited to see movement."

### 10.3 Productivity — lovable
- Use case: find/search/analyze documents, emails, chats.
- Deeper need: **reduce time spent searching**.
- Emotion when met: feel **effective, empowered, and less annoyed** ("I'm annoyed spending half a day collecting things just to answer an email").

### 10.4 Productivity — undiscussable: addiction
- Fear that **AI makes me addicted** — using it even when not needed, "**let's ask AI**" as the default.
- The tension: "**if you're addicted and use more time, how did it save you time?**"

### 10.5 Productivity — undiscussable: environmental impact
- A **PM** raises **environmental impact** as "a really big debate" — conscious of it even for small tasks: "to save you two hours… says a PM."

### 10.6 Hypothesis 2 — Cognition / creativity use cases
- **Planning features** and **ideas for automation** (a **tech lead**).
- **Discovering different solutions**.
- **Discover new architecture options** (a **software engineer** — permitting easier implementation / bug-fixing).
- **Substitute other roles for creating POCs** — outsource "less creative but still essential work" (e.g., UX text) to get there quickly.
- **Identify the proper problem statement / get inspired** — deeper need: **focus on the right thing** ("doing the right thing vs. doing the thing right").

### 10.7 Cognition — lovable feelings
- When you "do the right thing," the feeling is **satisfying** and **confident** ("my teammate always says 'satisfying'").
- AI acts as a **brain partner** giving more options — but "**not embodied**."

### 10.8 Cognition — undiscussable: wasted effort and quality doubt
- Fear of "**spending time for nothing** and then regretting it" — that the output "**won't be relevant**" and "I should have done it myself without the machine."
- Reframed as **insecurity/doubt about quality** and **inability to trust the output**.

### 10.9 Cognition — undiscussable: the hollow feeling / cognitive erosion
- More options first felt **complete and confident**, then **hollow**: "**am I losing my critical thinking capabilities?**"
- The recurring tension: **"Am I getting dumber or smarter by being helped by the LLMs?"** — cognitive erosion — "a tension I always experience."

### 10.10 The secret agenda — AI-generated session notes
- Xin Yao reveals she'll **photograph the sticky notes, feed them to AI**, and send **session notes** so participants needn't wonder what the stickies meant.

### 10.11 Hypotheses with no coverage
- **Decisions / agreement** (agents have **agency** = "acting/deciding independently") — no coverage.
- **Responsibility / ownership** (trust often comes up in AI debates) — no coverage.
- **Teamwork / collaboration** — no coverage.

### 10.12 Shared understanding — clarifying user intent
- **Architects**: interview the client to **clarify user intent** and reach **shared understanding**; they use LLMs to **prepare and filter interview questions**.
- Contrast: another group (in a different session) does **all their user research with LLMs** — "they're not talking with the customers anymore."

### 10.13 The isolation theme
- Substituting other roles for **POCs** can lead to **potential isolation** — the industry requires working with product, designers, developers.
- With AI you get **reactive answers, not proactive opinions**; "**opinions matter — it's not facts**," there can be **multiple truths** and an array of perspectives.
- You get "the **LLM's summary of diverse perspectives**, but not from **biologically embodied beings**" you can debate — a clear **social isolation** risk.

### 10.14 Learning to stay relevant vs. cognitive decline
- Multiple roles use AI for **learning and discovery "to stay relevant"** — which sits in **contradiction with cognitive decline**.
