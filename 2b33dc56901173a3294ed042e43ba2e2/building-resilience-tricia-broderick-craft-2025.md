# Building Resilience - Tricia Broderick (Keynote Outline)

> Tricia Broderick (co-author of *Lead Without Blame*, 25+ years in leadership) on building resilience "when crap goes down" — no magic checklist, but **four pieces of the puzzle**, each with something tangible to do.

---

## 1. Compassion — concerned care for self and others

- "It's business, not personal" is a quote from a mob movie — stop it; compassion belongs at work. Labeling a group a "team" doesn't make it one. Progression: **individuals → group (cooperation, a relay race, my part/your part) → team (collaboration, build it together) → high-performing/self-organizing**. Leaders must learn to **stop being the hub** (the desert of control). Build compassion through **intentional collaboration** — connection makes you invest in others' growth and well-being.

## 2. Confidence — belief in abilities (yours and others')

- The "Queen of Hearts" card trick: "please pick" phrasing let her force any outcome — that's **manipulation, not leadership or trust**. Don't manipulate people toward the answer in your head (it might be wrong, and it kills two-way trust). Be **intentional about building teaching situations** — she had others build/teach courses; the result was better than she'd have built, and it grew mutual confidence. "You stay the expert if you keep acting like the only expert."

## 3. Courage — engage in the risky unknown

- Courage is personal and situational (you can't just tell someone "be courageous"). Overhearing "Is Tricia your token female?" she got angry not at the praise but at what her colleague *didn't* say. As someone with privilege/power, ask **"what is my silence saying?"** — that reframe makes it easier to speak up (Dan North's "About time" support after her keynote announcement).

## 4. Complexity — explore and embrace the bigger picture (learning)

- Learning is hard, and it's the very thing complexity demands. Five inhibitors: **learning has baggage** (goals driven by perception — a "10-second freestanding handstand" she couldn't even kick up to; know *why* a goal is your goal); **it means change** (non-linear results; "I love change that I initiate"; don't overwhelm — 18-month release → "feasible daily deploy" terrified her team); **it means unknown** (Mount Stupid / Dunning-Kruger — she never learned *how to fall*; take exploratory steps for curiosity, not check marks); **it takes time** (find a **community** for support); **it means failure** (don't game metrics to fake a check mark — you stop learning).

## 5. Payoff

- Recognize **unexpected achievements** along the journey, not just the goal — people remember your *impact* on them, not whether the project shipped on time. She hit the 10-second handstand at exactly 18 months ("you won't if you stop") — and has essentially never done it again, because **resilience is the journey, not the check mark**; there is no "done." She closed by attempting a live handstand on stage. "How you build resilience is by being resilient."
