# Diversity: Two steps forward, one step back - Group talk (Panel Outline)

> A D&I panel moderated by **Gabriella Machkolár**, with **Noémi** (junior PM, Tesco), **Arina** (PM, EPAM; starting a Women in Big Data chapter), and **Zach Pendleton** (chief architect, Instructure). Title reflects that progress isn't a straight line.

---

## 1. Why Diversity Matters

- Create a **safe place to speak** about it (Arina). The most **diverse teams performed best** — best ideas, widest range of experience — it drives **business outcomes**, not just morality (Zach). Different perspectives mean there's always something to learn (Noémi).

## 2. AI as Risk *and* Help

- AI can **entrench exclusion** ("AI is what you teach it" — Amazon's hiring tool downgrading résumés containing "women's"). Praise for the **EU AI Act** flagging HR use cases as high-risk and requiring **humans in the loop**. Use AI carefully and critically — it can help *or* hurt.

## 3. Widening the Pipeline

- If you keep targeting the **same people**, you get the **same numbers** — broaden who you target. Don't just tell employees to "learn AI on your own time" — candidates from diverse backgrounds often have **caregiving constraints**, so provide **learning at work**. **Lead by example** (mentor/sponsor; you're not alone). Hiring senior roles only externally **inherits other companies' bad diversity decisions** — invest in teaching/learning to **promote from within**.

## 4. Loneliness, Pressure & "Hard Work"

- Being the only woman/foreigner/person of color in the room is isolating — representation (Tesco hiring more women) helps; **loneliness intensifies at senior levels** where competition is higher and sponsorship scarce. Underrepresented people feel **pressure to prove themselves** ("put in 10,000%"). Cancelled DEI programs "told us which companies never cared." On a KPMG finding that **hard work** now tops leadership skills for women: worrying because "hard work" is unmeasurable and biases toward "people like me" — get **concrete, measurable** performance criteria. Diversity as a **metric** must stay **merit-based** — hire for **perspective**, not to hit KPIs, by widening the pool.

## 5. Practices, Culture & Neurodiversity

- **Start early** (Tesco's "Girls' Day" for high-schoolers; girly-toys-vs-Legos), offer **part-time for every role** (life stage, caregiving), emphasize **human connection** over pure formal training, and coach a **blended leadership style** (assertiveness, "how to interrupt"). Advice to minorities: **be brave, be the role model, you are not alone**; seek mentoring/associations.
- **Cross-cultural teams** (dev in Hungary, product in UK) need dedicated communication time, in-person meetings, and agreed **ground rules**; never "everyone co-located except one person." On conflicts, **move up a level** to intent. **Neurodiversity:** educate on the topic, clarify expectations, and make **accommodations** (e.g. a quiet "silent-train" office corner for ADHD). Pledge: women ask for one AI opportunity within a month; men act as **allies**.
