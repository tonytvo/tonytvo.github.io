# AI & I: The Architecture of Our Identity – Xin Yao (Workshop Outline)

> An interactive workshop by socio-technical architect **Xin Yao** on *who am I?* when knowledge — our former core competency — is being commoditized by AI. Rather than a talk, it models "unlikely, awkward, high-stakes conversations" experientially, "making knowledge relational, not transactional."

---

## 1. Check-in — The Collaborative Calculator

- Five fist-finger questions surface common tensions: **daily AI use**; **excited by efficiency yet still time-deprived/addicted**; **cognitive boost vs. dependency/erosion**; **solo efficiency vs. missing social grounding**; **role/responsibility ambiguity**. Most raise multiple fingers — these ambivalences are what the session unpacks.

## 2. Connection Before Content — the VIEW Model

- The first connection we need is **to ourselves**. Draw an **intention compass** (one word in the middle — Xin Yao's was "courage").
- **VIEW** (Joe Hudson) — score your *intention* on each: **V**ulnerability (speak the scary truth / how willing to be seen), **I**mpartiality (a clear want without an agenda / how willing to be influenced), **E**mpathy (be present with another's emotion without taking it on), **W**onder (curiosity without needing the answer).
- Practice: trios discuss "**What about AI is making you feel weird or wild about being you?**" Popcorn reflections surface fear, purpose, anger, suspicion of outsourcing critical thinking, and **nostalgia** for the flow of creating. "There is no safe space until we make it safe" — the first person who risks being seen creates it.

## 3. Identity Architecture — Head / Heart / Gut

- Threatened identity collapses into **binary** thinking (Matrix "Am I the one?"); the four F's (fight/flight/freeze/**feed** — "just one more prompt"). Leaving an old identity behind requires **grieving**.
- Three circles around the pulsating "who am I?":
  - **Head = capability** — role, craft, skill (what fear-based AI marketing targets).
  - **Heart = capacity/connection** — impact, resources, support, the bigger picture (best skills can still yield mediocre performance without it).
  - **Gut = core** — safety *and* pleasure ("you rise only to the height of your lowest system"; which parts do you do out of fear vs. enjoyment?).
- **Craft is only ~1/3 of our humanity.** Identity is a never-ending **recalibration**, predicated on **emotional fluidity** — we make bad decisions to avoid uncomfortable emotions (FOMO signing up for the AI course).

## 4. Conversation Café — Possibles / Lovables / Undiscussables

Small groups model (event-storming style) their real configuration:
- **Possibles** (blue): roles + AI **use cases** in imperative form ("I, the PM, use AI to analyze user research"); mark collaborative ones.
- **Lovables** (green): the **deeper logical need** beneath a use case (five whys) + the **emotion** felt when it's met.
- **Undiscussables** (red): the elephants — worries, dilemmas, uncomfortable truths.

## 5. Themes & Tensions (harvest)

- **Productivity** use cases (research, documentation, code, debugging) vs. undiscussables: **addiction/over-use**, **environmental impact** — "if you're addicted and use more time, how did it save you time?"
- **Cognition/creativity** (planning, discovering architecture options, POCs, finding the right problem) with feelings of satisfaction/confidence — vs. fears of **wasted effort/quality doubt** and **"am I getting dumber?"** (cognitive erosion), and **social isolation** (an LLM gives a summary of perspectives, not embodied opinions to debate).
- Learning/discovery "**to stay relevant**" sits in tension with cognitive decline.

## 6. Integration

- Consume → digest (model your messy reality) → **integrate** (silent reflection via a feedback form; session notes to be AI-generated as an "Amazon-style narrative brief").
- Closing: **in the age of AI, answers are cheap — questions are the differentiator.** "If productivity is the trees, what is the forest for you?"
