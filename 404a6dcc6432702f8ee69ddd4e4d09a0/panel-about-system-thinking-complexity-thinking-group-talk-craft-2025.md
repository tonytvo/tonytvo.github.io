# Panel about system thinking, complexity thinking - Group talk (Panel Outline)

> A dense panel — **Dave Snowden** (complexity science/Cynefin), **Diana Montalion** (systems architect, *Learning Systems Thinking*), **Daniel Terhorst-North** ("happy consumer of both"), hosted by **Nigel Thurlow**.

---

## 1. Systems Thinking vs. Complexity Science

- **Diana:** systems thinking = focusing on how **relationships produce effects** and patterns (not engineering discrete parts). **Dave:** the clue is in the words — "systems *thinking*" vs. "complexity *science*"; complexity science **bounds and partly invalidates** systems thinking (like quantum mechanics to Newton). Systems thinking splits into **general systems theory** and **cybernetics** (Ashby → modern systems thinking; **Bateson** challenged it); complexity splits into **computational** (Santa Fe, "abstraction without context") and **anthro-complexity**. Systems thinking assumes **design without emergence**; anthro-complexity uses **emergence as a response mechanism** (manage actants/interactions, shift energy gradients). Key disagreement with Diana: focus on **interactions/actants** vs. the **individual** (seen as culturally North-American/N-European).
- **Daniel:** relationships between things are more interesting than the things (Donella Meadows); Cynefin domains are **attractors/aspects**, not boxes — any non-trivial system has overlapping characteristics. **Dave's critique of systems dynamics** (Meadows/Forrester/Senge): limits the number of feedback loops and turns **archetypes into categories**; "leverage points" imply you can *engineer* the system.

## 2. Root Cause & Reductionism

- **Reductionism's real error** isn't breaking things down (you must, to the lowest coherent granularity) — it's assuming **the whole derives from the parts**. In a **complex adaptive system there's no linear material causality**, so root-cause analysis is "a waste of time" and causes **retrospective coherence** (makes systems worse); root causes exist only in **ordered/manufacturing** systems (Goldratt's TOC). Software wrongly assumes a manufacturing/flow model. **Diana:** don't assume there *is* a knowable root cause — "the call is coming from inside the house" (the problem is inherent in what we value); fixing one problem spawns new ones. **Daniel:** Google's famous outage "root cause" (mudslide + fire + severed cable) → "we'll take those odds."

## 3. Ashby's Law & Language

- **Requisite variety** (if internal variety < environment's, the environment dominates) is **too linear** — "people are messy," so you need a more nuanced model. Ashby builds on **Shannon's restricted view of information**; human information (pheromones/trust, "information has momentum" per new materialism) exceeds it. Post-9/11 counter-terror work showed symmetric governments out-pointed by asymmetric threats (fix: citizens as sensor networks). **Diversity** matters (92% of devs are male) and our reductionist/command-control habits fail us even at defining "system" and "complexity." Language solidifies ("the Constantine moment") — insights born in metaphor calcify into dogma.

## 4. AI: Complication, Not Complexity

- Consensus surprise: **AI makes things complicated, not complex** (fast perspective/inference, algorithm-dependent, not relational-over-time). Humans reason **abductively** (a chess grandmaster "remembers a game that never existed" — recall, less effort), AI reasons **inductively** — and most software is written for inductive, not abductive, reasoning. Dave's new program: **measure abductive capability** in orgs as an executive early-warning (are humans still developing higher capacity?). Daniel's rant: **follow the money** — real AI (ML, image recognition, neural nets) is now underfunded because everything chases **LLMs**, which are "a really useful translation tool at scale" that is "always hallucinating (bullshitting)" — sometimes usefully.
