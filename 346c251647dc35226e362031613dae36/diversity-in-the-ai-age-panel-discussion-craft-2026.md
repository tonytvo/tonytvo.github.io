# Diversity in the AI age – Panel discussion (Panel Outline)

> A panel (moderated by Gabriella) on whether the rise of AI is changing diversity in tech — or recreating the same patterns — with leaders from Tesco, MSCI, IBM, and the conference organizers.

---

## 1. Why Diversity Matters (intros)

- Panelists (Katalin/Tesco, Medea/organizers, Rozália/MSCI, József/IBM) share personal stakes: nonlinear careers and part-time advocacy, conference practices (mixed-price tickets, speaker-ratio targets), "diversity brings different perspectives that serve the company," and a Hungarian podcast host who realized 20 episodes in that **all his guests were men**.

## 2. Is AI Changing the Picture?

- Meetups on AI dev still show ~few women per 100 men. AI **democratizes knowledge**, but access wasn't the real barrier — the missing thing is **role models and mentors** (STEM Sisters: a mentee valuing seeing a woman already in a tech role and feeling *empowered*). This is a **human** problem AI doesn't fix.

## 3. It Starts in Education & Parenting

- Personal story: a teacher literally told the boys to come forward and the girls to sit in the back ("girls can't learn this") — and 30 years later, a daughter had a similar experience (a strong show of hands in the room). Encourage **daughters and sons the same** (bravery, cooking, crying) — self-confidence is planted early; boys should also be told they can be kindergarten teachers, dance ballet, do chores.

## 4. Concrete Practices

- **Part-time positions** advertised deliberately (Tesco went from one part-timer to part-time-by-default); **flexibility** (working from a café between school runs) lets women pursue and *rise* in roles without fear of losing family time.
- **Diverse interview panels** — counter the "hire a mini-me" bias; different genders/nationalities/backgrounds yield better, independent decisions.
- **Women-in-AI meetups / Girls' Day / STEM Sisters** — safe spaces build confidence ("men apply knowing 5 of 10 criteria; women hesitate at 8 of 10"). But a tension: **positive discrimination** risk (US legal climate; and women internalizing "I'm only here because of a quota" / imposter syndrome).
- Sales/technology roles are "double barriers" (travel, client dinners vs. work-life balance) vs. shared-service centers (opposite ratio) — hence **sponsorship** to bring more women in.

## 5. Q&A — Bias & "Female" Management

- **AI + diversity:** biased training data → biased decisions → the case for **AI/data governance** and diverse datasets.
- **Is female management different?** Consensus: **more similarities than differences** — good leaders are leaders (human-focused), bad ones are narcissists, regardless of gender. We over-generalize by gender ("maybe 1% of men and 0.5% of women act 'aggressively masculine'"). Women often bring **different topics/perspectives** to a management team (positively received) and, bearing more family responsibility, a **cooperative** edge. Concern: AI's visible role models are all male CEOs (Altman et al.) — we need to build **female role models**.
