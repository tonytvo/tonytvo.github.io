# From idea to impact – Panel discussion (Panel Outline)

> A cross-track panel (product, data, UX/UI, AI, leadership) previewing the Compass AI & Tech Summit — discussing how AI changes building successful products, moderated by Mefi with organizers/track leads.

---

## 1. What AI Really Changed

- **Clean/trusted data matters more** — dashboards and answers now come from AI, so trustworthy data is paramount.
- **A K-shaped economy:** teams that use AI well go up, those that don't go down — a new separation of companies masked by hype/noise.
- **Pricing AI products** is an open, possibly-unviable business-model question ("who can afford it, how long?").
- "We're **drowning in information but starving for wisdom** — AI accelerates that." The missing thing is **judgment/clarity** on which of the 10 generated options to pick.
- **UX/UI:** designers are wary AI-designed screens worsen UX, but prototyping and research **analysis/synthesis** get much faster (no belief in synthetic users).

## 2. The Reality of Acceleration

- **Faster:** prototyping (weeks → hours/minutes), tighter learning loops.
- **Slower/harder:** turning individual productivity into **institutional** improvement (Gergely's keynote) — divergent individual output makes chaos grow exponentially → **AI slop**; needs controls/quality guarantees.
- AI "makes evident what we swept under the rug" (data quality). The real challenge: measuring **value created**, not productivity — "AI makes you run faster, including into the wrong direction," so you need clean data + fast user insight to run the *right* way.

## 3. Clashing Roles

- Overlap across product/design/engineering is increasing (a product+tech pair can build in days). But **build-to-learn ≠ build-to-earn** (Marty Cagan) — "you can write a UI in 5 minutes, that doesn't make you a designer." Domain knowledge and **critical thinking** matter *more*; you can't judge AI output in a field you don't know (crappy UI, insecure code you can't see).
- **Two organizations:** one where a non-technical PM now over-promises to frustrated engineers (AI breaks things); one where product + engineering already collaborate (AI helps tremendously). AI may "replace" those who won't learn to communicate/learn — a hard but fair lesson.

## 4. Does AI Replace People?

- Split view: some say **fewer, more effective people**; others say tasks (welcome to lose) vs. jobs (AI gives *more* job). Historical framing (traffic lights created new jobs; The Guardian's 200-year data) — technology tends to create more work than it destroys, but the **learning curve is faster** now.
- **Risk profile shifts:** 3 engineers running agentic processes means each holds 1/3 of org knowledge (vs. 1/20) — though "the agent still has the knowledge if it's codified in markdown."

## 5. Open Questions & Q&A

- Panelists want answers on: how to start with **agent harnesses**, how **interviews/hiring** change, bridging individual→org productivity, discovery/prioritization when you can build anything, and "where will we be by October?"
- Biggest misconception in **building AI products**: slapping an AI chatbot on your product vs. rethinking how AI solves real customer problems 10× better. Most overhyped: some say **AI-generated art/music**; one panelist argues **nothing** is overhyped ("it's underhyped and scary").
