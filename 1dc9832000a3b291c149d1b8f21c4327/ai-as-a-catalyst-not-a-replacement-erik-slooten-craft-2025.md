# AI as a Catalyst, Not a Replacement - Erik Slooten (Talk Outline)

> Erik Slooten (EY executive, ex-CIO; 90s network engineer who lived through the internet's arrival) — a motivational talk on what *we* do as people as the AI wave arrives.

---

## 1. This Is Already Reality

- American and Chinese dev environments are half agents (agents in meetings answering budget/code/lifecycle questions). His team (devs, data scientists, architects, analysts) uses AI for coding/review/debug — **idea → implementation went from months to weeks** (a high-profile security case in 3 weeks with 2 people + 5 agents). Saudi Arabia launched a fully-AI clinic (doctors behind the scenes managing agents); AI-run hotels; people already ask Perplexity medical questions. AI won't take all jobs, but it **replaces the need for certain things to be done by people.**

## 2. Craft Becomes a Niche

- Like handmade furniture, **handcrafted code becomes a premium niche** — most lower-level coding done by agents, **supervised by people** who understand collisions, memory leaks, quantum vs. binary. Like a carpenter with laser-guided (even AI-infused) tools, you stay master of your craft **surrounded by software at your service** — stop thinking you write everything yourself; manage your world differently.

## 3. Data Science Is the New Foundation

- The internet exploded data; AI exploded it more (79 → 181 zettabytes by year-end). **Learn data science deeply** ("the new cement of everything") — you'll have more tools *and* mountains of construction material (generate custom synthetic data). Other thoughts: there's **no OSI layer for AI** yet (it doesn't fit as "just another application"); **agents are your new colleagues** (befriend them, learn to prompt each differently — prompt engineering = communication skill); OpenAI's device bets will **disrupt the smartphone**; **ethical code matters** (mistreating AI teaches bad behavior; codify conduct; filter/reset bias — easier in AI than people).

## 4. Reinvent Yourself ("Be a master, not a hamster")

- Personal story: a heart problem at 41 became a catalyst to take six months off and learn to surf — proof you can **reinvent yourself anytime** (he went network engineering → software → cybersecurity → AI). Don't cling to what you thought you'd do forever. **Be a master** of your software environment, your client's expectations, and the AI workshop — an **orchestrator** combining different AIs while warranting quality, trust, testing, and ethics. "Writing C is a trick; *this* is mastery."
- Q&A: hallucinations come from **conflicts/discrepancies**, not synthetic-vs-human data (clean data via data science); AI's biggest limits are **lack of common sense / world-awareness** and resolving conflicting requirements (and AGI raises ethical questions before technical ones); learn data science via statistics first + hands-on internships; wrap AI in a **"responsible AI" framework** (monitored repos, bias/compliance/ethics checks, refreshed over time); LLMs will move back to a **foundational** role while **agents** do the critical, self-questioning coding; you can't de-bias the whole internet, so **guardrails** matter as much as clean data.
