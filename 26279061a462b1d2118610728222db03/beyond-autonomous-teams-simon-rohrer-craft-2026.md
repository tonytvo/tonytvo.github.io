# Beyond autonomous teams – Simon Rohrer (Talk Outline)

> Simon Rohrer (co-author of *Sooner Safer Happier*; Saxo Bank) argues we should move *beyond* "autonomous product teams" — up to **agency + coherence** and a **nested-and-networked** organization of **value centers**.

---

## 1. "Who Are We?" — The Expanding "We"

- The word **"we"** carries different scopes (me, my office, my org, my industry). As we reason about teams and AI, we must expand who "we" means.

## 2. "Autonomy" Is a Container Concept

- Socio-technical roots (Tavistock coal-mining studies): mechanization killed the miners' autonomy; efficiency returned when they **rediscovered** it. But **autonomy = "self-law"**, which isn't quite what teams want.
- "Autonomy," "sustainability," "quality," "product" are **container concepts** — you fill them with your own meaning (vs. Bernard Williams' "thick" concepts like *courageous*). Teams need **agency** and to understand their **web of dependencies**, not "unlimited authority" — they need **clear boundaries to act freely within** (João Rosa).

## 3. From Product → Value Centers

- "Product" is also a container concept; Scrum's "product" comes from the *new new product development game* (standalone products), which doesn't fit complex orgs where no single team ships a "mortgage."
- *Sooner Safer Happier* framing: **Better Value Sooner Safer Happier** (quality / flow / agile-not-fragile / happier customers-colleagues-citizens-climate). Evolved from "product teams" → "value streams" → (with Chris Matts: software value streams are one-offs, not manufacturing) → **value centers** (inspired by *cost* centers, but you deliver value, not sunk cost) — one nested word for squad/tribe/department.

## 4. Value Designs Organizations

- Your **web of dependencies is the shape of your value** — some coupling is **accidental** (removable), some **essential** (Vlad Khononov: there's always coupling left; you won't DDD it away). "Molecular value shapes": hyperscalers have tiny, standalone atoms (two-pizza teams); a trading platform (80 teams, one product) has a huge shared kernel (orders, prices, risk); a universal bank is more complex still.
- Extends Conway (Ian Mael: **funding** also shapes systems; Sanchez & Mahoney: products design organizations) → **value designs organizations** at all levels (fractal), constraining the **autonomy ↔ coherence** trade-off.

## 5. Viable Systems Model — Five Questions

- Every value center (at every level) answers: **What value am I delivering? How do we coordinate? How do we fit together? What's out there for us? Who are we (identity/purpose)?** — some "I" questions, some "we" questions, in a feedback loop with the environment.
- **"The purpose of the system is what it does"** (Stafford Beer) — you don't get to choose your purpose; if your A&E makes people sicker, that's your purpose now.

## 6. Both/And, Not Either/Or

- The duck-rabbit (Wittgenstein's aspect perception): it's **both**. The real trade-off is **autonomy *and* coherence** (Fetzhkov: dove of freedom + glue/lubricant), **order *and* disorder** (Stacey) — governing constraints suit order (align early), enabling constraints suit disorder (cohere later).

## 7. From Autonomy → Agency + Coherence

- **Agency** is a better (thicker) word than autonomy, balanced by **coherence/communion/relatedness** (Gilligan, Bakan, Juarrero; even Deci & Ryan's self-determination theory includes **relatedness**, not just autonomy). Other languages already qualify it: German "*semi-autonomous*," Dutch/French/Hungarian "*self-steering*"; "AI agent" is a bare loanword everywhere.
- Strategy/sensemaking is **top-down *and* bottom-up** (Roger Martin's call-center example); platforms are **enabling constraints** (do more), but some governance must be **central** (Novo Nordisk's security incident forced centralized policy).

## 8. Agency for AI Agents Too

- The METR "most important line" (AI task horizon rising) reduces agency to "hours without human intervention" — but agency is thicker. The same five questions apply to your **5 agent tabs**: what value is each delivering, how do you make them coherent, what boundaries (harness/context) let them act freely? Sharing **skills/context** at team→department→org levels expands the "we."
- Closing moves: **product → value**, **autonomy → agency + coherence**, **autonomous teams → nested-and-networked organization**. Purpose is continuously negotiated up and down, hopefully coherent. "Who are we? We are the software engineering community."
