# Shaped by Demand: the power of fluid teams - Daniel Terhorst-North (Talk Outline)

> Daniel Terhorst-North on organization design: "build the best structure we can to do the best work we can — **for now**." The challenge shifted from getting 8 people pointing the same way (cross-functional teams) to **80 or 800**.

---

## 1. What Is "the Best Work"?

- Not just **delivery** (features). Also **discovery** (solution- and problem-space), **the work about the work / Kaizen** (continual improvement of the *system* of work — automation, learning/teaching), **BAU** (keep-the-lights-on: reports, compliance, audit), and **failure demand** (work that should never have existed — outages, reconciliations, plus all the follow-up). Mandatory spend (BAU + failure demand) vs. **discretionary spend** (delivery + discovery + Kaizen).

## 2. Debunking "Stable, Long-Lived Feature Teams"

- The received wisdom sold by scaling frameworks. **"Stable"** comes from **Tuckman** (forming/storming/norming/performing) — but his meta-study was **50% therapy groups, 25% HR training**, only 25% actual working groups; later studies (US military) show the stages happen **concurrently and fast** (a shared-culture consultancy team performs in *hours*). **"Long-lived"** assumes homogeneous work (false at Bank of America — 30–40 legacy systems, work changing quarter to quarter). **"Feature team"** ignores Kaizen/automation/tech-debt work. So instead of shaping demand to fit teams, **shape teams to fit the demand**.

## 3. Demand-Led Planning

- **Identify *all* the demand** (much hides in plain sight — HP LaserJet firmware team drove **failure demand from 95% → 40%**, turning 2 hours/week of discretionary time into 3 days). **Allocate discretionary spend** (how much feature/Kaizen/discovery this quarter — sliders can move but **none goes to zero**). **Pitch the demand** (sponsors pitch to everyone in the room — 30 to 200 people; fly them together, it's palpable).
- **Constraints:** *governing* (3–10 people; teams must be **fully autonomous** — never blocked on another team; have skills/knowledge/experience/relationships/permission/resources; ≤3 teams per **bumblebee**, ≤3 bumblebees per team) and *enabling* (min/max movers per quarter, min/max tenure) to encourage knowledge transfer. Then people **self-organize** against the constraints (iterate 2–3 times), each new team declares a **BHAG** and a team name.

## 4. Making It Work & Payoff

- Prep: evangelize up and down (get the "suits" comfortable shaking 100 people in a bag; expect tears/tantrums — one rage-quit PM later said "you were right"). Coach sponsors to pitch; use skilled **facilitators**; big room, natural light, fruit + coffee; **manage energy, not time**; follow up with an **academically defensible Likert survey** (people report high connection to work/mission).
- Teams measure themselves with **throughput, lead time (a range), WIP**; reviews are **forward-looking** (how much is left, not what's done). **Identity is in the program team** (~60–80 sweet spot), the squad is transient (like early Spotify tribes). Management manages a **system of work with people in it**, not people; appraisals/on-call live "out of band" (guilds). **Autonomy through alignment**: alignment without autonomy = autocracy; autonomy without alignment = anarchy — give the *minimum* constraints so people can do good work (mission command). At John Lewis (90 → 500 people), planning a quarter for 100 people eventually took **half a day**.
