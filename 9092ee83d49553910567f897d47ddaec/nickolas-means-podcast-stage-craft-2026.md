# Nickolas Means – Podcast Stage (Conversation Outline)

> ABK / *Leadership Anonymous* interviews **Nick Means** (engineering leader at Shopify; famed for engineering-disaster storytelling) on reinventing yourself when the ground shifts under you.

---

## 1. Change Is Multi-Layered

- Change is interesting because **part of you must stay the same** (core principles/values) while **what you layer on top** — how you express those values — changes with the world.
- Finding your identity: look for the things you **keep pulling out of every role** — those are your core values. For orgs, counter generic value statements with "**but do you actually do those things?**" (aspirational vs. real). Shift from **adjectives to verbs**.

## 2. The Hardest Thing to Change & Grieving

- The hard-won skill of "expressing complex ideas in semantic languages so computers obey" is now **commoditized**. But the higher-order work — **delivering business value** — hasn't changed.
- Those who **hung their identity on writing code** are having the identity crisis; product engineers map through more easily.
- Coaching language: you must **mourn the person you're no longer** — you can still enjoy code, it's just less valuable as a professional skill.

## 3. Grief, Pain, and Learning

- Maps every coached developer onto the **Kübler-Ross change curve** (denial/anger/bargaining/depression/acceptance) — non-linear ("Opus 4.6 came out and everyone got sad again"), and moving **very fast**, week by week.
- **"Humans are bad at learning and growing unless we're in pain."** The industry "touched a hot stove"; the pain won't stop soon (token-maxing → token economy).
- **Non-determinism changes debugging**: it's no longer necessarily *your* mistake when the computer does something weird — which is actually harder.

## 4. Story as the Through-Line

- Means tells disaster stories (Three Mile Island, Air France 447) because **humans are pre-wired to learn from story** (oral tradition predates writing).
- Framing/statistics: in 2024 you were **more likely to win an Olympic gold than die in a plane crash** — "all our problems are really a framing problem" (unless you're actually in pain).
- **"The rules of aviation are written in blood"** — a learning culture that gets to the bottom of every failure (not true of software broadly).
- Advice: **be curious about the world outside software** — there's almost always an analog; and AI is great at finding analogies / playing devil's advocate ("talk me out of this rut").

## 5. Finding Your Authentic Next Chapter

- Avoid the hype train by keeping a **list of mistakes you've learned from** and knowing **why you lead** (you don't get control — just a big carrot and stick).
- Means' anchor value: **retaining humanity in the industry** amid pressure to move faster and pull humanity out of the SDLC. "We spent decades pretending we're not humans."

## 6. Leadership as a Creative Practice

- Requires **time and space**: "if you find yourself trying to look busy, you're doing it wrong." Over-managing creates messes; negative space enables a real *practice* of leadership (vs. being reactive).
- **Journaling / morning pages** (Julia Cameron's *The Artist's Way*): unclog the subconscious; answers surface overnight. "Journaling for people whose neurodiversity won't allow a journaling practice."
- **Call your shot** (Yogi Berra): be willing to do "weird stuff for 6 months" because you know the results — much of leadership is invisible (1:1s, coaching).

## 7. Q&A Highlights

- **"Turning from a coder into an agent-manager to keep my income — won't people end up doing jobs they hate?"** — Reframe from "good programmer" to **"good problem solver"**; code was always a means to delivering value. If you *truly* just want to write code, life-/safety-critical domains (heavily specced, no AI) still exist — if that doesn't appeal, you were a product engineer all along.
- **"Do people take this as personal failure?"** — Yes, *everybody* ("what made me valuable isn't valuable, ergo I'm not valuable"). The antidote is realizing it's **universal** (Kübler-Ross) and finding human connection.
- **Ambiguous identity aids adaptability** ("I'm a person who likes sport" survives an ankle injury better than "I'm a great runner").
- **Monday question for leaders:** "Have I spent enough time building software agentically myself to have the empathy to lead others through this change?" — put your own oxygen mask on first.
- Team-building as a **party-building game** (collect complementary skill "cards"); leadership is solving the creative problem of making them click, then not clinging as it evolves.
