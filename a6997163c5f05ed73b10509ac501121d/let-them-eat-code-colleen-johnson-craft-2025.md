# Let Them Eat Code - Colleen Johnson (Talk Outline)

> Colleen Johnson (CEO of ProKanban.org, co-founder of ScatterSpoke) on why developers spend only ~30% of their time coding — and how to reclaim focus by making the invisible visible.

---

## 1. The Cost of Lost Focus

- Interviewing engineering leaders surfaced **developer focus time** as a top pain point — yet it's often "measured" by just asking devs to guess. The startling stat: developers spend **70% of their time not writing code**. Put in dollars ($150k avg salary), that's ~$105k/dev/year lost — **$1M for 10 devs, $10M+ for 100** — on top of the sunk cost of hiring/recruiting great engineers. The time goes to unnecessary meetings, status reports, endless standups, and **waiting** (for decisions, dependencies, prioritization, direction).
- The pain compounds: **burnout** (busy but not progressing), **context switching**, low morale, frustrated PMs/leadership/finance, and ultimately the **customer** (slow delivery, poor quality, lost trust). The core move: **make the invisible visible** so you can control the amount, order, and duration of work.

## 2. The Six Wastes of Software Development (Lean)

- **Overproduction** — extra "just in case" features/small bets that aren't needed. **Partially done work** — started and never finished; the longer undeployed code sits, the more it costs (no ROI). **Defects** — pull you away and force context switching (~20% lost per switch). **Extra process** — Scrum ceremonies (planning, retros, standups) across time zones are context switches. **Waiting** — delays in decisions and handoffs, cross-team dependencies. These are why only ~30% of time is coding.

## 3. Manage Capacity & Make Priorities Visible

- **"Working on everything, finishing nothing"** comes from too much **WIP**: cognitive overload (40 tickets, which to pick?), context switching, and thrashing between dev/test/review. On a typical board people hold 2–3 tickets each — the extras just sit. Instead of always pulling from *To Do*, **move things to Done first** and help teammates who are overloaded. The **airport analogy**: planes landing faster than gates open up → planes stuck on the runway with angry passengers. **Match WIP to team capacity** with **WIP limits**. ("If you can lean, you can clean" — at the limit, do research/testing/reviews, don't start new work.)
- **"Everything is urgent, so nothing is important"** (security teams have some of the highest turnover for this reason). Make it easy to see the single most important next thing. **Color-code tickets by feature** and check alignment: if the #1-priority feature has zero tickets and a #5 feature dominates the board, that's a conversation. Tie work to **business outcomes / strategic goals / OKRs**.

## 4. Make Age Visible & the Four Flow Metrics

- **Stagnant work** ages out of sight/out of mind; picking it back up means re-learning requirements, resolving stale merge conflicts, and coping with changed priorities. Fix: **make work item age visible** (plugins, or just put the start date in the title and sort oldest-first). Standups become "this ticket's been in progress 6 days" → swarm it, hand it off, or split it. Great for surfacing Scrum **carryover** (maybe a 2-week sprint is the wrong timebox).
- **The four flow metrics:** **Work in progress** (actual count, matched to capacity), **Throughput** (raw count of items done per period — not story points), **Cycle time** (elapsed time start→finish), and **Work item age** (elapsed start→today). The first three describe *finished* work you can't change; **age is actionable right now**. All the data already exists in your tools — just make it visible.

## 5. Reducing Distractions & Uncertainty

- Build a system that makes it easy to **say no** to new work by showing current WIP and the **trade-offs** of switching. Leaders must **model** this (push back on a PM breaking capacity). The **sheep story**: running a lamb in a harness failed; mounting a raised feeder so it stood on its hind legs to eat won grand champion — **know the outcome, cut the noise**, drive to the goal. Reduce uncertainty with **probabilistic forecasting** (Google Maps for real-time re-routing) instead of estimation. Frame everything as **trade-offs** ("stop this and it slips to next week") so stakeholders make informed decisions. Focused developers build better products; distractions aren't just annoying — they're **expensive** (lost talent). Start by making **WIP and age visible** and having better trade-off conversations.
