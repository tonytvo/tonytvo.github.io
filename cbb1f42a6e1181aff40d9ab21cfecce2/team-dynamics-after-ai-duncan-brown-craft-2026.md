# Team dynamics after AI – Duncan Brown (Talk Outline)

> Duncan Brown (software engineer turned team-builder; former Head of Software Engineering at the UK's *Incubator for AI*) on what AI does to **teams and organizations**, not just individuals — via two forces: **amplification** and **hybridization**.

---

## 1. The Small Giant & a War Story

- The team's core text ("the era of the small giant") pairs an **old idea** (small teams < 7 are great) with a **new** one (amplify each person 10× with AI).
- **Project Hummingbird:** a 2.5-person team amplified a developer (John) to ship a working constraint-solver prototype in weeks. But the real work fell on the **delivery manager (Esther)** at the boundary with reality — information governance, cybersecurity, cross-department politics. The software "worked"; politics killed it.
- Then other teams started depending on Esther too — a **human bottleneck** that is really a **signal about failing to change the world**. Feedback loops are nested (individual: seconds; team: days/weeks; **org: months**), and orgs are set up **unbalanced** and hard to rebalance (identity, politics, HR/budget).

## 2. Amplification Is Seductive but Dangerous

- Like a volume knob: drive an amplifier too hard and you get **clipping/distortion** and blow the speakers; and you must **balance** yourself against the band. Those balance choices are traditionally **negotiated within teams**.
- Why the fixation on *making more*? Two ideas:
  - **From us:** the belief that software is end-to-end an **engineering/production problem** (Accelerate/DevOps) — great downstream, but smearing it upstream into the *negotiation with reality* (product) is an open question (Dan Shipper's "dark software factory," Steve Yegge's "Gastown" — less human negotiation, less feedback, less intention).
  - **From outside (Jurassic Park):** **Dennis Nedry** — the loner 10× dev whose power is **gatekeeping** and 2M lines only he understands. AI amplification **deepens silos** and drives teams apart.

## 3. Naur — Programming as Theory Building (1984)

- Software is a **byproduct**; the program text is secondary to the **theory the team holds** about the program. Without a living theory you can't modify it — and if you remove the people, the program **dies**.
- This favors multiplicity, diversity, negotiation, **psychological safety**. The **dark software factory** dispenses with psychological safety and takes **theory-building out of our control** — but that boundary between us and the world is where **choice and intention** live.

## 4. Hybridization — AI Tools Instead of Colleagues

- Story of the designer **Adam** + tech-lead: a weekly design-dev catch-up around the artifact saved the "build the **right** thing" money and made the theory **durable/redundant**. Hybridization replaces the colleague with a tool — a **one-way** skill flow into the engineer's head, "a travesty of collective intelligence."
- It **decays**, because the colleague had their *own* external feedback loops: Adam contributed 30–40 patches to the gov.uk design system; AI-only teams contributed **zero** — every day the shared tool gets a little more mediocre. You end up as Nedry, staring at your own reflection.

## 5. Becoming — Joe's Story

- **Joe** (bootcamp → senior, years of Ruby) at an all-in-amplification scale-up copy-pasted Elixir from ChatGPT to seem "credible," felt like an imposter, and **quit** — the company lost a brilliant future AI-engineering leader because they believed the cliché. AI looks very different from the **economic lens** (upstairs: amplify, hybridize away, save money) than from the **team level** (rigor, quality, growth).

## 6. Conclusion

- These forces have **cliché** and **balance-sheet legibility** behind them; blindly accepted, they yield **unlimited mediocrity**. Widen the frame from individuals to **teams**: hold space for diversity of perspective, intention, and skill.
- **Managers/tech leads must shape their org's AI policy, build the feedback loops, and adapt** — executives who don't even understand DevOps can't. Trust your people; carry the torch for **collective intelligence**. "Augment yourselves in order that you can continue to augment one another."
- Q&A: don't anthropomorphize agents (they don't live in the world, so people get infinitely higher priority); help juniors by valuing **proactive experimentation** over "read K&R"; you never have more leverage than when about to quit.
