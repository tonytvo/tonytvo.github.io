# The Psychology of Software Teams - Cat Hicks (Talk Outline)

> Cat Hicks (PhD quantitative psychology; studies developer thriving) on "how are we doing at being technical?" — and how the industry does **bad psychology** about developers.

---

## 1. The Visibility Gap

- Managers vs. developers on "is technical *effort* seen here?": **88–90% of managers** say yes, but **<24% of developers** agree — a perception misalignment (worse for lateral roles: DevOps/platform/infra). Widespread **distress**: ~43–45% report **AI skill threat**; higher **code-review anxiety** → each unit increase raises avoidance behaviors ~40%. Developer voices: "so much of my work never sees the light of day"; "I'm not convinced leadership thinks we're people."

## 2. The Brains-in-Jars Model

- The **bad mental model**: developers are isolated, fungible, antisocial "brains in jars" producing discrete units; social/collaborative work is invisible. Leads to **learning debt** (orgs signal *don't* share learning → juniors learn to keep heads down) and **brittle productivity** (short-term "number go up" that shatters catastrophically when things go wrong) vs. **resilient productivity** (built for long-term mastery).
- Why: **performance ≠ learning** (Bjork — good long-term learning can *degrade* short-term performance); **performance orientation** is brittle; **brilliance belief** kills inclusive innovation. **Cat's Law:** *the more a team fixates on short-term performance, the less it can protect the foundation long-term performance depends on.*

## 3. Psychological Affordances (breaking up with brains-in-jars)

- Our minds seek **affordances** ("seed and soil" — adaptive beliefs need a social context that reinforces them). The **LABS** model / **developer thriving**: **Learning culture, Agency, Belonging, Self-efficacy** (+ motivation). Measured on 10,000+ people: a strong positive link to (self-reported) productivity — each increase in dev thriving ≈ **25% gain** in productivity, holding across 12+ industries, roles, and demographics.

## 4. AI Skill Threat vs. Contest Culture

- **Identity threat** + **contextual sensemaking**: does "being a developer" still make sense under GenAI? A pre-registered, inclusively-recruited RCT-grade study of ~5,000 devs: in a **contest culture** (constant weeding-out contests from the brilliance belief) you're **~2× as likely** to feel AI skill threat vs. a thriving/learning-and-belonging culture. AI skill threat is **distinct from impostor syndrome** (contest cultures drive both, but even non-impostors feel more skill threat in a contest culture).

## 5. Software Is Pro-Social; Evidence You Can Change It

- Reject "developers are antisocial" — the work is inherently **pro-social/collaborative** (90%+ of devs say "it matters that my work helps people"). The **10x-engineer myth** (traced to 1960s–70s task-time studies) doesn't hold up: analyzing **cycle-time data (11,000+ people, 216 orgs)** with hierarchical models shows **heterogeneity is the expectation**, and a developer's own past cycle time doesn't predict their future average — so resist individual blame/praise and easy benchmarks. Finally, a **real-world distributed RCT** on **code-review anxiety** produced statistically (and clinically) significant reductions plus gains in self-compassion/self-efficacy — proof psychological environments are **measurable and changeable**.
