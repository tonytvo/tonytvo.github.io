# Inside the Brain of the Top 1% AI Companies – Márton Szabó (Talk Outline)

> Márton Szabó (specialized hiring agency partnered with Sequoia; works with Lovable, Synthesia, etc.) shares what top-tier AI companies actually optimize for when hiring.

---

## 1. Skills Get You In; Judgment Gets the Offer

- Opening story: the *same* GitHub engineer was an instant yes at one AI company and an instant reject at another — not error, but **different judgment being optimized for**.
- Thesis: **skills are table stakes; judgment wins offers.** Judgment = making decisions under pressure and **owning** the outcome (you can't deflect to "my manager told me to"). AI won't take responsibility — you must.
- New interview formats: two coding rounds (one AI-allowed, one not) — they don't care if you can write code (everyone can now), but whether you **understand** it and can **defend your decisions**; or solving an actual company problem end-to-end. AI made the **first draft cheap**; your judgment makes AI strong.

## 2. Seven Engineer Archetypes

- "Software engineer" is dead as a title; top companies hire for **~7 archetypes**. Three detailed:
  - **Forward-deployed engineer** (judgment under constraint) — makes the solution work inside the *client's messy reality* (legacy, politics, deadlines). Palantir-originated (~2012); these people often become founders. OpenAI acquired a deployment company (~$4B, ~150 FDEs) because **the bottleneck isn't model access, it's customers being unable to use the product.** A good product engineer isn't automatically a good FDE.
  - **Product engineer** (agentic) — orgs deleted the **PM handoff**; when code is cheap the *loop* is the bottleneck. Engineers now own **discovery → iteration**, measured by **business impact, not output** ("lowered runtime 10%? nobody cares").
  - **Applied AI engineer** (judgment under uncertainty) — most startups are "wrappers"; value comes from making the same available models **reliable, cost-effective, and trusted** (no hallucinated contracts). Researchers usually don't transfer into these seats.
- Since AI gave designers *and* front-end engineers each other's tools, the era demands **end-to-end ownership** — big tech delivering the same with fewer engineers.

## 3. The Hiring Funnel Is Flooded

- CVs full of keywords/"10 years of Java" mean nothing (everyone AI-optimizes them). **It's not AI rejecting your CV — it's people**, because you optimized for the wrong things.
- Scale: 11 Labs reported **~31,000 applicants in one month**, ~143 hires in H1. Stand out with **judgment and demonstrated value**; connections and events help.
- **Follow the money:** OpenAI → deployment; YC's "AI-native services companies" (companies spend 4–5× more on services than tools); Sequoia — "model access is not the moat, **domain judgment** is." AI didn't make engineers obsolete — it **made judgment the whole game**.

## 4. Q&A / Advice

- **Juniors:** build **side projects** to be judged on value (and maybe earn money).
- **CV:** keep the Java, but write **outcomes/value created** next to it — humans focus on that.
- **Get an interview:** have **connections**; come to events.
- **Will big/old orgs adopt this?** Already are — "talent density": fewer but better engineers producing the same. AI won't erase jobs; it changes the jobs and the judgment required.
- Now is a great time to **build your own thing** — software is cheap and easy if done right.
