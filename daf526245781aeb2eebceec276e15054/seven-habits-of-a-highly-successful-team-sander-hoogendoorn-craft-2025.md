## 1. The Problem

- A 20-year-old daily-deals e-commerce company started on an Excel spreadsheet, grew organically, forked open-source systems it can't upgrade (ERP 8 versions behind), scattered/synced data everywhere → **technical death** (all time spent maintaining, none innovating). The real cause is **never the tech** (monoliths and legacy are fine — the Pantheon still stands) — it's the **organization/culture** always pushing new features over refactoring, and the **innovator's dilemma**.

## 2. The Seven Habits

1. **Prioritize pragmatically (70/20/10):** 70% innovating toward the "dot on the horizon" (a clear mission), 20% small features on existing systems, 10% keeping the lights on. A weekly **tech board** (managers + him) filters every idea: does it reach the goal? can we do it (13 people)? is it small enough (break it down)? do we *need* it now? — re-prioritizing weekly; the rest goes to a "someday/maybe" (really the bin).
2. **Kill complexity** with **small steps** (continuous renovation — "keep the shop open"; Gall's Law: complex systems can't be built from scratch). Most work is in Cynefin's **complex/chaotic** zones (unknown unknowns) → **experiment** and allow failure ("be a *cwtch/*coddiwomple — travel purposefully toward an unknown destination"); make steps smaller than you think.
3. **Own the work (autonomy):** anyone can pick up anything from the boards. Software is a team sport ("the smallest unit of ownership is the team" — Charity Majors); autonomy is hard to teach (like "draw the owl") — people learn by doing.
4. **Simplify to amplify** (Dee Hock: simple purpose/principles → complex intelligent behavior; complex rules → simple stupid behavior). They removed rules (when/where/how/what tools; office one day/week) — like a crossroads where **removing traffic lights** made people communicate. They dropped Scrum/sprints/retros/PO/stories/estimates/pull-requests/blame — "we're our own product owners = **product engineers**."
5. **Communicate/collaborate:** knowledge-sharing over private knowledge — **event storming**, lots of **pair/mob programming**, weekly **lean-coffee** sessions.
6. **Deliver continuously:** cycles kept shrinking (RUP months → DSDM/XP/Scrum weeks → **continuous delivery/deployment**, ~**40–50 deploys/day**). Tiny changes → rich feedback; "if it hurts, do it more often, bring the pain forward" (Humble); **the bus metaphor** — missing the bus is fine if the next is in 5 minutes. Automate everything. (His laptop/PowerPoint dramatically crashed mid-talk — he improvised without slides, fittingly.)
7. **Have fun:** team outings help, but motivation comes from **purpose, mastery (learning together), autonomy** (Dan Pink). Not everyone likes change (his late mother went to the same French campsite for 25 years — and that's fine) — "old keys won't open new doors"; **you** are the only one who can ignite change, never stop learning, and "take your mom out to dinner more."
