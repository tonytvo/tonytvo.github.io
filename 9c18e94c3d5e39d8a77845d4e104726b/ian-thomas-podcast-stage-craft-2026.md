## 1. Anatomy of an AI-Native Org

- For 30 years software orgs were **pyramids**, with most head count in the middle doing **translation work** — turning business intent into something engineers/computers can execute.
- AI makes that **translation work cheap** (not just code cheap), so the pyramid narrows in the middle. Provocative line: *"if a manager isn't contributing to the why, the what, or the trust system that holds the how, it's hard to say why they're there."*
- Pushback: it's a **false prophecy** that managers vanish — their *role and size* change, but the persistent problems (cross-team collaboration, unclear business logic) remain. "It's usually a people problem, not a tools problem" — don't fix it with an anonymous double-blind feedback tool; teach people to have tough conversations.

## 2. iPhone Anti-Snatching & Hardware Lock-in

- Apple's instant-lock-on-theft feature; iPhones are already hard to resell because parts are **cryptographically bound** to the device (display/cable/components need certified-shop authorization).
- Host's true story: tracked down and recovered a stolen iPhone via Find My; the phone bricked itself and announced it was stolen. Counterpoint: a night market will "solve" DRM in a minute.

## 3. Meta Glasses & the Instagram Chatbot Exploit

- Delight (trying Meta glasses — whispered background music, weather, Hungarian support) vs. dread: the same week, Instagram accounts were hijacked by **social-engineering Meta's support chatbot** into sending a verification code to an attacker's email and disabling MFA. "This is not the future — this is the present."
- Broader gripe: at Google/Meta/Netflix you **can't reach a human** for support (fired or never staffed), unlike a bank.

## 4. Enthusiasts vs. Skeptics (Charity Majors)

- Charity Majors' article: AI enthusiasts and skeptics are both responding to real existential threats but have **stopped talking to each other**, creating silos. *"Enthusiasts race against time; skeptics race against entropy."*
- Fix: sit down, talk, and **share the whole story — including the bad news** (people over-share AI wins). Follow balanced/critical voices, not just fanboys.
- Meta-skill: **hold two opposing truths at once** — be enthusiastic about AI *and* know it's imperfect; disagree with someone and still respect them. (Ties to Hungarian "us vs. them" polarization.)
- Kent Beck's coinage adopted: the **"unpredictable genie"** that solves everything "in an unpredictable, non-deterministic way."

## 5. Curiosity, Inner Child & Intuition

- Recurring podcast theme: **curiosity** should drive software, personal growth, and relationships ("the death of love is when you stop being curious about your partner").
- "Kids just know" — they're in tune with intuition (too hot for long pants, when they're full). Reconnect with your sense of amazement (jump rope at the playground; watch a snail — a snail moves ~2 km/h, per the "Roost" slow-social app).

## 6. "What to Do When You're Not Detail-Oriented"

- Practical, "Captain Obvious" advice worth doing: **identify the skills you're bad at** by noticing dread (the Sunday-4pm stomach pain before a Monday presentation), then deliberately do those uncomfortable things frequently. "You don't hate Mondays, you hate your job."

## 7. Data-Center Water Use

- Google/Meta/Amazon are addressing (and PR-spinning) data-center water consumption; recent framing suggests the water issue was *over-hyped* — met with healthy skepticism ("who paid for that study?").
- **Drinking water** is becoming the most sensitive resource; desalination is increasingly viable but energy/cost-bound. "Technology will solve it" — maybe.
