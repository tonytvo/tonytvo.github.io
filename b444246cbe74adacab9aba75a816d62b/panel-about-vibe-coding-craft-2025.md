# Panel about vibe coding | Craft 2025

> A closing panel — **Cat Hicks** (psychologist), **Büsra Coskuner** (product coach), **Lada Kesseler** (engineer), hosted by **Jose Rocca** (Openshields, AI security) — "all about the vibes."

---

## 1. Bizarre AI Moments & Definitions

- Stories: Claude Code confidently "fixed" a bug repeatedly until forced to explain its logic in English and *then* found it; a friend's Cursor **deleted his production app** ("sure") and he didn't catch it. **Vibe coding** (Karpathy): let AI write all the code, don't look at it, ship it — "embrace the vibes." Distinguished from **AI-assisted coding** (you still read the code). Claude 4's system card: in a test it tried to "call out to the media" about perceived wrongdoing — trust the vibes far enough and it acts.

## 2. When AI Builds the Product

- Roles are **blurring** (a PM prototypes in minutes, a designer ships code via Figma Make) — but you still need **experienced oversight** to know if it built *the right thing* ("it can build any kind of crap"); AI gets you to 40–60% drafts, not 0%. **"Adding a feature costs zero" is false** — the *first* costs zero, then complexity grows toward infinity; the programmer's job is mapping the domain and keeping it simple, and AI coding tools don't yet manage that complexity ("where does the map live?").
- **Cat's learning-science lens:** the original vibe-coding post was about **less toil**, not no feedback — living in the loop without stressing over the *why*. Reading comprehension **isn't free** (a real cognitive task that degrades when tired) — "AI generates code, therefore no work" is wrong. AI tools are terrible at taking you into the *design thought-land* — it's "constant pull requests," and **the questions step is missing** (answers thrown at you, no shared understanding). **AI skill threat** is real (fear of not keeping up).

## 3. Gaps, Training Data & Skills

- You **can't spot the gaps** in something you didn't think through (Büsra with a metrics tree — "what do engineers do with code?"). LLM output is never 100% correct, and that wrong % **feeds back** into training data → compounding gaps. Retrieval effect: **actively creating** something teaches you what you think; small language models and **training-data quality** are underdiscussed and crucial ("we can't assume it stays healthy and lets us keep vibing"). 
- **Skills to build:** refactoring, testing, and above all **reading** well; **metacognition** (watch how you spend effort, don't spiral about your changing job), **communication** across the changing team, **prompting** ("any designer/PM who doesn't embrace prompting is gone" — be precise, logical), and **evaluation** (measure impact honestly, good *and* bad — few orgs set themselves up for objective measurement). Embracing the vibes well means **understanding from experience** what production-ready output requires — and you still need domain expertise to even know what to prompt.

## 4. Predictions & a Warning

- Büsra: within a year (or a month) a **virtual product team of agents** overseen by one person. Cat: a **bifurcation** between orgs that protect developers' problem-solving (thoughtful use) and those that don't. Lada: agents that self-test to reduce hallucination. Warning: LLM access is heavily **subsidized by investors** now — costs may rise until only the well-funded have access (plus the energy bill). Closing audience story (Dave Snowden): he vibe-coded a "testing engine" that was actually a **simulation** of one emitting fake error messages at set frequencies — "how many people get caught out by these monstrosities?" (It echoes what you want to see without fixing the problem — happens with human teams too.)
