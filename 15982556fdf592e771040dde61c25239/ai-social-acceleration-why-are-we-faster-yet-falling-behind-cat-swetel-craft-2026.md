# AI & Social Acceleration: Why are we faster yet falling behind? – Cat Swetel (Talk Outline)

> Cat Swetel (GM, Nubank; science & technology policy background) on why, if AI makes us so productive, we're all so tired — a socio-technical lens on the AI era.

---

## 1. There Is No "Push Through"

- Everyone's exhausted; the instinct is "power through AI adoption, then things go back to normal." **This is the new normal — there is no slowdown** (like the internet making work follow you everywhere). Temporary morale boosters (hackathons, cute t-shirts) won't power an entire era; you need **sustainable ways to cope**.

## 2. Rosa's Acceleration Theory

- Hartmut Rosa: **technological acceleration → social change → faster pace of life**, mutually reinforcing. Social norms shift with it (fast food didn't create family time — it densified life with multitasking).
- But **not everything accelerates**: a baby still takes 9 months, a cold still takes its time. **Pace mismatches** create pain — short job tenure (~14–18 months) vs. 9-month pregnancies → "we'll pay you to freeze your eggs"; everyone getting a car → **traffic jams** (individually faster, collectively slower).

## 3. AI Has No Rhythm

- Humans have circadian rhythm; teams have cadences (standups, planning). **AI is continuous and has no rhythm**, so integrating across it (and across teams) feels janky.
- **Agentic dev over-materializes**: prompt → a fully-built thing, skipping the divergence/convergence where humans iterate. A materialized design is **harder to argue with** and easily becomes a **commitment** (we hate turning things off, and agents rarely say "let's stop — we're done"). Agents need to learn what humans learned: **take smaller steps**.

## 4. Little's Law & Frenetic Standstill

- **Little's Law:** if the **arrival rate** rises (agentic dev generating prototypes) but the **departure rate** (value actually shipped to customers) stays flat, **work-in-process piles up** and things take longer. Research shows lots of *stuff* created but not much extra *value* shipped — plus quality issues (GitHub/AWS incidents) and regulator requirements can even lower departure rate.
- Rosa's term: **"frenetic standstill"** — doing a lot without traction. Caused by the **shrinking present**: when C-levels reason in weeks, every layer below compresses (weeks → days → hours), so the only "safe" action is proximate and low-impact — hence burning tokens on unimpactful things.

## 5. Now What — Human Value

- **Intuition** is something machines lack — invest a little in honing/listening to it (even "silly" experiments like tarot to notice and interrogate your gut). No real downside.
- **Be in your body** — experience your natural rhythm rather than "living through GPUs."
- **Locus of control** — this smart room can affect the industry. Question the assumption of **perpetual growth** ("what grows continuously? cancer"). Every past tech ("internet/microwave will give us leisure time") densified life instead — so **capture AI's value as leisure**, and steer this transition (Grace Hopper reference) toward something more **equitable**.
- Both/and: **everything is exhausting *and* exciting** — you're allowed multiple contradictory feelings.

## 6. Q&A Highlights

- **Resist acceleration without losing competitiveness:** prioritize ("does this move the needle?"), anchor on real-world fixed timelines (regulators), treat a materialized design as *not* automatically a commitment, and **leave the building** (museum, bike ride, coffee — together) for creativity and energy.
- **One harmful practice:** layoffs — she'd prefer **fewer working hours** over cutting 40% of staff; leisure has societal value ("unions 2.0").
