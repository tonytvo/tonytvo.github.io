# Behavior and attitude, the new Agile approach - Nigel Thurlow (Talk Outline)

> **Nigel Thurlow** — former **Chief of Agile at Toyota**, creator of **Scrum the Toyota Way**, co-creator of **The Flow System**, CEO of the Flow Consortium — English, lives in Texas (hat and boots, per a promise to his wife). A deliberately fast, funny, and alarming talk: we don't have a **methodology** problem, we have a **behavior and leadership** problem — plus a rapid-fire AI tour.

---

## 1. The State of Agile (2025)

- Riffs on his "Jobs" talk: **no more certification/PDF factories, made-up roles** ("no such thing as a flow master"), and the Cynefin "big picture" now behind a paywall ("a secret society").
- The **transformation myth:** the Agile Manifesto "beautified into sainthood" to prescribe how to transform a company — "**it's just a philosophy.**" And the misuse of **culture** — "**culture is a product of our behaviors.**" (Nearly rewrote the talk after Kent Beck's keynote to focus on his **distributed leadership / psychological flow / team science** work.)

## 2. Leadership Issues

- We don't need more methods/tools/techniques — **we need better behavior, starting with leadership.**
- **The reality of change:** if you earn **$1M+/year (some execs $45M)**, you don't have a problem — the system you built works, so **without significant fear/pain, nobody changes.**
- **America runs on incentives** — bonus, status, power, income — with short horizons (**OKRs**, one to three months) and metric-worship: the **McNamara quantification fallacy** (focus on the metrics, ignore contradicting information — coined by an ex-Ford president).
- **Leadership isn't a job title — you earn it by building followers** (followership). The test: **"if you stopped paying them, would they still follow you?"** Lean/agile fails because orgs **desire** change but aren't **designed** for it, and leaders "are all right" and make every excuse not to change.

## 3. The AI Threat (fast and alarming)

- **Timeline:** transistor (1947) → AI (2017) → ChatGPT (2022) → **agentic AI in 2 years.** **Yoshua Bengio** (neural-network Nobel laureate): AI's task capability **doubles every ~7 months**; "~5 years from a lot of trouble." **Geoffrey Hinton**: "a very special point in history where everything might totally change at unprecedented scale."
- **Party tricks that matter** (he's 62, ex-COBOL/JCL/punch-tape, "can't code anymore"): a **Christian-rock Disney song** built in **90 minutes** (Suno for music/vocals from adapted *Lion King* lyrics, LTX Studio for storyboard/imagery); a **Grok** self-portrait + **HeyGen** persona video; and **Gemini writing ~800 error-free lines in 2 minutes** from a "stupid little prompt." *"It's not what it wrote — it's the fact it wrote it."* Yes it **hallucinates** ("put glue on pizza"), "but plenty of humans write bad code and we don't remove them" — and **it'll get better faster than us.**
- **Agile coaches are "the dodo"** — extinct unless they evolve. "Who is my customer? They don't want agile coaches anymore." The "field of dreams" fallacy. What leaders actually want **lean** to be: cut headcount, deliver faster, more profit, more status (Goldratt "pandered to their greed — it's all about money").
- **Physical AI** = robotics + AI + sensors/actuators/manipulators + world simulation:
  - **NVIDIA Omniverse/Cosmos** + **Accenture** + **KION** — warehouse **digital twins** (Mega blueprint; robots perceive/reason/plan/act in an Omniverse twin; simulate infinite scenarios before deploying). 20M+ hours of simulated video free on GitHub; chips as low as **$350**; Hugging Face.
  - **Pegatron** (Fortune 500) digital-twin case study; but "**AI won't solve bad leadership**" (Boeing and a collapsing Japanese carmaker use digital twins too).
  - **Wetnee Robotics** laundry-folding robots; **Waymo** in Austin (**20% of rides**, "no tip, doesn't text and drive, clean, doesn't drive like an idiot"); **Tesla Optimus** ("Musk's next product, not cars — China will eat him for lunch"; they rode in a Chinese-built MG EV).
  - A **self-learning LLM with zero input data** (self-trains — "scary as hell"); **Google AlphaEvolve** writing/testing/debugging its own code and iterating — **"AI doing agile all by itself."**
- **Jidoka** (**Sakichi Toyoda**, ~100 years ago): "**machines with a human-like intelligence**... the human should never be removed; machines should serve humans" ("there is a beauty in the human touch"). Agentic AI is **probabilistic, not deterministic** — perceive/reason/plan/act autonomously.

## 4. What to Do

- **Change behavior, not mindset** (**John Shook**, lean: "we don't change how people think, we change how people behave — we give them alternatives"). Stop the **"seductive allure of neuroscience"** (brain pictures make people believe anything) and **"mindset"** talk (pseudoscience — an *individual* has a mindset; you can't *collectively share* one). Appeal to leaders' **actual desires (P&L)** — why the big consulting houses thrive.
- **EAST** framework to "sell to leaders": make it **Easy** (desired = default), **Attractive** (design incentives in), **Social** (reciprocity), **Timely** (stress immediate costs/benefits).
- **New coaching roles** (beyond "353 of Scrum"): **ethical-risk coach**, **cyber-risk coach** (though "AI auditing AI" already exists), and **project-turnaround specialist** — pull the metaphorical **andon cord**: stop/slow completely, **stabilize** (remove variation), address **tech debt** ("not a problem — a bad decision, usually by management"), eliminate red tape/non-value-add, make **quality** central, run **weekly stakeholder reviews**. Coaches evolve into **orchestrators/conductors** bridging worlds.
- **Agentic = giving agency = power of attorney** — you **outsource the decision**; you're no longer the owner. Dark examples: **Anduril's Roadrunner** autonomous weapons ("smart weapons vs. dumb weapons"), a Chinese factory robot beating its handler, and **human enfeeblement** (we forget how to change a tyre, read a map).

## 5. Q&A

- **Tasks AI can't replace?** **Trades** — plumbers, electricians, mechanics (until cars go autonomous/electric). Diversify your **skills portfolio**; beware **human enfeeblement**. "To become valuable, learn skills beyond coding."
- **Keeping AI in check with its own output?** He doesn't fully know — it's an **arms race** ("information is the new oil"), unfettered, with LLM API hacks/threats already happening → must be **governed at governmental/legal level**; we must become experts at controlling it.
- **Organizational Behavior Management / behavior change + balancing AI?** Give people opportunity to **exploit their knowledge/skills**, allow mistakes and support failure — the **andon cord** = psychological safety to stop work; "where's the andon cord in software when tech debt piles up?" The lean view: **AI is augmentation/assistant/tool** — the danger is **greed** (billionaires now crave power, not money).
- **Which of the seven AI levels are we at?** No idea — can't keep pace; most worried about **coding/development ability**; "we're very close to **AGI**." Start studying what it means for your **life/family/community**, not just tech, and **reskill**.
- **When to say "no more AI" in a project?** "No answer — 8 seconds ago **your boss** already decided; you won't have that decision, and that's the sad part."
